Design, Analysis & Simulation of Solar sail propulsion system for cubesats.

CAD model of the Solar Sail with Three Axis Stabilized sails which can propel a CubeSat Platform. The Software used for this design purpose is ‘CATIA V5’.

Solar sail analysis using Ansys software

In order to carry out a primary analysis and get an authentic result, Following assumptions have been considered:

1) The material nonlinear of film is neglected, and treated as linear material. 2) The films are treated as one material. The aluminium layer above film is neglected. Because the aluminium layer is very thin and very light compare with the film. 3) Only the solar radiation pressure is considered, other planets’ effects are all neglected. And the solar radiation pressure is considered as a constant. 4) The temperature is considered as a constant.

3D Model rendering

Rendering of the CAD Model and gltf conversion using ‘BLENDER’ Software.

Results Obtained from Static Structural & Modal Analysis

The solar radiation pressure of 0.912×10-5N/m2 was applied to the solar sail, normal to the sail membrane. The temperature of 184°C was applied to the solar sail. The bottom of booms and the vertex of each triangle sail are fixed in all DOF.

OBSERVATION: The deflection is mainly depending on the solar radiation pressure, the temperature almost has no effect on the deflection of sail. This is because the expansion coefficients of the material of boom and sail are very small in the design.

Mission simulation in Systems Tool Kit (STK)

Mission Objective: 1)To model a Solar sail mission trajectory to the vicinity of Sun-Earth L1 point. 2)The mission outcome includes Continuous Sun (Solar storm) monitoring without the interference of the Earth and provide enhanced warning of such storms to allow preventative action to be taken to protect vulnerable systems.

Trajectory analysis using MATLAB

Here we describe an interactive MATLAB script named ss2d_opt.m that can be used to analyze and optimize two-dimensional, heliocentric solar sail trajectories between the orbits of Earth andVenus (inner transfer) and between the Earth and Mars (outer transfer). In this script, the heliocentric planet orbits are assumed to be circular and coplanar. The optimizationof these steering angles is performed using the SNOPT nonlinear programming (NLP) algorithm.
